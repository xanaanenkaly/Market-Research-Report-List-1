<p><p>Power distribution cabinets are classified into three market types based on voltage levels: low-voltage, middle-voltage, and high-voltage. Low-voltage cabinets, typically under 1,000 volts, manage electricity for residential and commercial applications. Middle-voltage cabinets operate between 1,000 to 35,000 volts, serving industrial and utility sectors for greater electrical demand. High-voltage cabinets, exceeding 35,000 volts, are essential for transmission networks and large-scale power distribution, ensuring efficient management of electricity across extensive distances and maintaining system reliability.</p></p>
