<p><p>The food coloring market comprises several key types, each derived from natural or synthetic sources. Caramel color, created through sugar heating, provides a brown hue to foods. Capsanthin, extracted from peppers, gives a red tint, while turmeric offers yellow coloration. Carotenoids, from various fruits and vegetables, deliver vibrant shades. Annatto, sourced from achiote seeds, adds a yellow to orange color, and carmine derives from cochineal insects for red. Red beet imparts a deep purple, spirulina adds blue-green, and chlorophyll provides green, with "others" covering less common options.</p></p>
