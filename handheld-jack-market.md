<p><p>The handheld jack market includes three primary types: pneumatic, hydraulic, and mechanical. Pneumatic jacks use compressed air for lifting, providing quick and easy operation, typically seen in automotive applications. Hydraulic jacks utilize fluid pressure to lift heavy loads, offering stability and power, commonly used in construction and industrial settings. Mechanical jacks rely on manual force, often using a screw mechanism, making them suitable for lighter tasks and portable applications. Each type serves distinct needs based on lifting capacity and convenience.</p></p>
