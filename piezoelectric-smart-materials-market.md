<p><p>The piezoelectric smart materials market comprises three primary types: ceramics, composites, and polymers. Ceramics, known for their high piezoelectric coefficients and thermal stability, are widely used in sensors and actuators. Composites combine polymers with ceramics to enhance flexibility and mechanical properties, making them suitable for applications requiring durability and lightweight designs. Polymers, being more adaptable and cost-effective, offer versatility in applications ranging from medical devices to consumer electronics. Each type contributes uniquely to the growing demand for advanced smart materials.</p></p>
