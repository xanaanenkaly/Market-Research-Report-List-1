The market features two primary types: P-PRP (Pure PRP) and L-PRP (Leukocyte-rich PRP). P-PRP contains a higher concentration of platelets and lower white blood cells, making it ideal for promoting tissue regeneration. In contrast, L-PRP has a higher leukocyte content, which may enhance the inflammatory response and aid in quicker recovery. Both types aim to improve functionality and alleviate symptoms in patients.</p></p>
